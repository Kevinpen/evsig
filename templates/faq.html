Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do you determine the most similar IDRs?

The most similar IDRs are those with smallest distances to the currently searched IDR. The definition of distance is Pearson correlation between the vectors of all Z-scores that comprise the evolutionary signature.

To find the most similar IDRs, we calculate the distance between the currently searched IDR and all other IDRs in the dataset. We list the top 10 with the smallest distances.

The list of most similar IDRs may differ from the adjacent IDRs in a global cluster plot (as in the data available in the “Cluster” section of this website).

2. How to interpret the evolutionary signature?

The evolutionary signature is a set of standard Z-scores quantifying the deviation of the evolution of molecular features in an IDR with respect to our null expectation.

As (a set of) Z-scores, the evolutionary signature is calculated by subtracting the mean of the simulations that form our null expectation (μ) from the value in the real IDR (x) and divided by the standard deviation of the simulations (σ). The formula for the Z-score is Z = (x- μ)/ σ.

Our work (Zarin et al., eLife 2019) has shown that IDRs with similar evolutionary signatures are associated with similar functions.
