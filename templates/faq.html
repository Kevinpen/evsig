Frequently Asked Questions

1, How are the most similar IDRs decided?

The most similar IDRs are those with smallest distances to the currently searching IDR. The definition of distance is Pearson correlation between the vectors of all Z-scores in an evolutionary signature.

To find the most similar IDRs, the distances of current IDR to all IDRs in data set are calculated, only the 10 with smallest distances are listed.

The list of most similar IDRs may differ from the adjacent IDRs in cluster.

2, How to interpret the evolutionary signature?

The evolutionary signature is a set of Z-scores, it is defined and calculated so that IDRs with similar evolutionary signature have similar functions.

Unlike structured regions of protein, intrinsically disordered regions are hard to assign functions based on amino acid sequences alone, and the amino acid sequences are usually not conserved in evolution. The paper (Zarin et. al, 2019) found that some molecular features are conserved in evolution of Saccharomyces cerevisiae.

As (a set of) Z-scores, the evolutionary signature is a measure of deviation, it is the degree of molecular features deviate from the simulated evolution. The calculation is subtract the mean of the simulations (μ) from the real value (x) and divided by the standard deviation of the simulations (σ). The formula for the Z-score is Z = (x- μ)/ σ. For all molecular features, the mean and log standard deviation Z-scores are calculated and divided in groups.
